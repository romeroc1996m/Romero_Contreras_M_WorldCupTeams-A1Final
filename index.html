USA

USA The United States' performance at soccer's biggest event has a mixed history. having taken part in the initial World Cup in 1930 and doing well there.

While the United States took part in the first World Cup in 1930 and then advanced to the Round of 16 in 1934, there was a significant period between 1950 and 1990 during which the country did not make the tournament.

Italy

ITALY Italy is among the most successful national teams in World Cup history, having taken home four different championships. The took part in 18 of the 22 games.

Italy placed second is Group G during the World Cup qualifying matches. The team participated in 18 of the 22 competitions, making it to six finals and finishing third and fourth overall.

France

FRANCE In 1904, the France national team was formally established. France participated in the first World Cup in 1930. They created history by becoming the first side at the World Cup to score a goal in their opening match when Lucien Laurent beat Mexico.

At the Euro 1984, their first European Championship in 24 years, they continued their impressive performance.

Canada

CANADA When teams in Toronto started playing by Football Association rules in 1876, Canada's men's soccer had its beginnings.

Canada had a record of one win, one draw, and two losses during World Cup qualifying. In the North American Group, Canada came in second place but did not advance. The amateur Canadian team competed in both Olympic Qualification and the Pan American Games in Colombia in 1971 during this time period.

Australia

Although having a short history of involvement in the FIFA Global Cup of Football, Australia has a rich sporting heritage and has won numerous world titles.

Australia had played 22 matches by the time they qualified for Russia 2018, becoming the 31st of 32 teams to do so. This was more than any other team had ever played in World Cup qualifying.
